BiasClear Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 20, 2026

BiasClear is a small, founder-operated service. This page explains what we collect on biasclear.com, why we collect it, and how we handle it.

What We Collect

  • Beta signup email addresses when you request API access.
  • Scan request content only for the purpose of generating a scan result.
  • Operational metadata such as timestamps, request identifiers, scan mode, and provider status.

What We Store

  • Beta signup emails are stored in a dedicated operational database so we can reply to access requests.
  • BiasClear's audit log stores scan metadata, score details, and privacy-safer signup metadata such as masked email and SHA-256 email hash.
  • The public health and stats endpoints expose service-level telemetry, not raw scan text.

What We Do Not Do

  • We do not sell your data.
  • We do not place secrets or private credentials in source code.
  • We do not publish raw beta-signup email addresses in public audit responses.

How We Use Information

  • To respond to beta access requests.
  • To operate the live scan service and monitor reliability.
  • To improve detection quality, stability, and documentation.

Retention and Deletion

Beta signup emails are retained for access management and outreach related to BiasClear. If you want your email removed, contact brad@biasclear.com.

Questions

Questions about this policy or data handling can be sent to brad@biasclear.com.
